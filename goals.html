Goal: Discover Myself

A collection of reflections documenting my professional growth and skill acquisition over time.

Portfolio 1: Dreams and Goals

My primary career goal is to architect highly scalable software systems that solve complex quantitative problems. The discipline required to reach this goal is something I have cultivated heavily outside the classroom. Recently, completing a rigid training block to run the Toronto Waterfront Marathon taught me that long-term success requires incremental, daily execution. I apply this exact endurance to my software engineering timeline, understanding that mastering technical architecture is a marathon, not a sprint.

Portfolio 2: Interests and Skills

I have a strong interest in generative AI and workflow automation. As Head Event Coordinator for YUSSC, I manage the logistics, safety, and budgeting for large-scale ski trips. This experience drastically changed my perspective on my computer science coursework: I realized that managing complex, moving parts in real-world events perfectly mirrors the logic required to design clean backend system architecture. Both disciplines require anticipating edge cases, managing resources efficiently, and ensuring user safety.

Portfolio 3: Values and Personality

I value analytical rigor paired with creative problem-solving. My current role as a Treasury Analyst requires strict adherence to quantitative accuracy and financial discipline. Conversely, my freelance work as a full-stack developer allows for a high degree of creativity in UI/UX design. My strongest professional trait is adaptability—I am able to seamlessly switch between the strict logic of mathematical modeling and the creative execution of modern web development.
